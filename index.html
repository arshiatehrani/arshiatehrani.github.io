About

I am detail-oriented and enjoy taking on challenges, using each one as an opportunity to learn and grow. As an M.A.Sc. candidate in Electrical and Computer Engineering (ECE) with a specialization in Artificial Intelligence at Queen’s University, and an Electrical Engineering graduate from Tehran Polytechnic (Amirkabir University of Technology) with minors in Control and Computer Engineering, I am passionate about leveraging advanced learning theory to solve complex engineering challenges.

My work focuses on driving digital transformation across clinical healthcare and power utility energy systems. I specialize in building trustworthy machine learning architectures, uncertainty-aware forecasting models, and LLM-based engineering agents to automate diagnostics and transparent control. I'm always eager to collaborate on advanced AI research, control systems, and innovative engineering applications.

2025 – Pres.

Interpretable Deep Learning & Feature Attribution in Healthcare

Arshia Esmail Tehrani (M.A.Sc. Thesis Research)

Queen's University, ECE Department

Engineered a safety-prioritized explainable AI framework (XGBoost & SHAP) for clinical rTMS treatment screening (N = 58), achieving a 1.00 Recall (zero-miss) for high-risk decliners under bagged LOOCV. Conducted rigorous statistical modeling (ANOVA, ANCOVA, non-linear GAMs) and a 4-cohort comparative analysis to mathematically separate active magnetic neuromodulation from placebo and delayed recovery effects. Developed a multi-seed Sequential Forward Selection (SFS) stability wrapper to identify global white matter hyperintensity (WMH) burden as a non-linear "vascular veto" and left-dominant prefrontal GM asymmetry as protective. Uncovered a clinical blindspot between subjective risk scores (HIS) and MRI-derived vascular damage, proving global brain integrity is an absolute physiological prerequisite for active magnetic neuromodulation.
